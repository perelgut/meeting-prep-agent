# SLIDE 3 — WHAT WAS BUILT

## The Meeting Prep Agent

A working AI application that:

- Connects to your **Google Calendar** and reads upcoming meetings
- **Researches** every attendee, agenda item, and relevant news topic in real time
- Accepts your **private interpretation** of what the meeting is really about
- **Synthesizes** everything into a five-section professional briefing
- **Downloads** as a Word document ready for the boardroom

&nbsp;

### Built in two days. Deployed on the public internet. Free to run.

# SLIDE 6 — HOW AN AGENT DIFFERS FROM A CHATBOT

## Two very different things

| | Chatbot / AI Assistant | AI Agent |
|--|----------------------|---------|
| **You give it** | A question | A goal |
| **It does** | Answers | Plans, searches, decides, produces |
| **Output** | Text on a screen | A document, an action, a decision |
| **Your role** | Prompter | Reviewer and approver |
| **Analogy** | A very fast librarian | A capable junior analyst |

&nbsp;

### An agent doesn't answer your question. It does your work.

# SLIDE 7 — THE HUMAN IN THE LOOP

## The agent proposes. You decide.

&nbsp;

Every research result is shown to the user **before it enters the briefing.**

The user can:
- **Accept it** — it proceeds to synthesis
- **Dismiss it** — it goes no further
- **Postpone it** — revisit it later

&nbsp;

> *"The agent proposes. The human is responsible for what gets used."*

&nbsp;

This is not a limitation. **It is a design principle.**

# SLIDE 8 — THE MOMENT THAT SURPRISED EVERYONE

## What the agent got wrong — and what that taught us

&nbsp;

During testing, a very public figure was entered as a meeting attendee.

The agent researched them thoroughly. Found accurate information.
Wrote up their background as an active, current participant.

**The person had been dead for years.**

&nbsp;

**The fix:** One sentence added to the agent's instructions:
*"First confirm whether the person is currently alive. If deceased, state this clearly."*

The agent never made that mistake again.

&nbsp;

### Common sense is not automatic. Once stated, it is perfectly followed.

# SLIDE 15 — THE ONE THING TO REMEMBER

## "The agent proposes. You decide."

&nbsp;

AI agents are extraordinarily capable at research, synthesis, and structured production.

They are not capable of judgment, context, or accountability.

Those remain yours.

&nbsp;

The workshop is built on this principle. Every design decision in the application reflects it. Every participant leaves with it as an instinct, not just a phrase.

&nbsp;

### The most valuable thing your employees will learn in two days is not how to build an agent. It is when to trust one — and when not to.
